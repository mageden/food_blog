  Recipe translated from video [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIKVZhXUl6A)
recipe:
  ingredient_groups:
    - items:
        - "400 g of wheat flour, type up to 550 - this is about 2 1/2 cups (250 ml)"
        - "250 ml of lukewarm milk"
        - "120 g of sugar - about 1/2 cup (250 ml)"
        - "80 g of butter, melted and cooled"
        - "30 g of fresh yeast -> 10g instant, 1 heaping teaspoon"
        - "4 egg yolks, size L"
        - "100 g of raisins"
        - "2 teaspoons of vanilla sugar or vanillin sugar"
        - "grated zest of one lemon"
        - "a pinch of salt"
    - name: "For the glaze"
      items:
        - "about 1 tablespoon of water"
        - "about 2 tablespoons of lemon juice"
        - "about 200 g of powdered sugar - this is about 1 1/2 cups"
  step_groups:
    - steps:
        - "Mix yeast with 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 cup of flour (60g) and 100ml of lukewarm milk. It should look like it has risen."
        - "Mix and set aside for 15 minutes."
        - "Add remaining milk (150mL), sugar (120g), salt, vanilla sugar (2 tsp), flour (340g), lemon zest, and egg yolks."
        - "After mixing it until incorporated, add the 80g of butter. It will form a thick batter that pulls from the sides of the bowl. Lastly, add the raisins."
        - "Set aside in warm place covered for 30 minutes (?84?)."
        - "While that happens grease bundt pan with butter and flour."
        - "After 30min transfer to tin. Dough should show signs of life, jiggle a little, and appear more elastic. Transfer by handfuls directly into the tin."
        - "Cover and leave to rise for ~30min. It should rise ~50% and appear domed at the top. It will not crest the edges of the bundt pan."
        - "Place in 350F oven, bake for 35m or until toothpick comes out clean."
        - "Leave to cool and then invert."
---

Notes
- After step 3 the dough was too dense. I added some extra milk, but it didn't look like the video. Maybe the flour was too strong and they were using more of a cake flour?
- Oven wasn't fully pre-heated, which I could tell since the bread wasn't that brown by the end of the 35 minutes. The internal temperature was 210, quite high for a enriched dough, and yet the final product was gummy when tested on a warm slice.
