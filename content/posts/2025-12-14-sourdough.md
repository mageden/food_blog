Process notes
- Fed started with 50% rye for 3 feedings prior and for levain. More than doubled, had not collapsed.
- Upped flour when adding salt from 25 -> 45g
- Used buckwheat for my 50g, also added 15g bran
- 72 starting temp for bulk, then 79 fold 1, 78 fold 2, ...
- Proofed for 4 hours, preshaped, then let rest 20 minutes. During preshaping it appeared a bit overproofed having roughly doubled
- When I checked it the next morning it had risen way above the banneton and was super sticky, sticking to the banneton causing it to collapse. Definite signs of it being overproofed. This is a great sign that changing the starter is the solution to getting better rises, rather than further manipulation of bulk timing/temperature
- Final dough was baked 20 @500 and then 25 @ 450. It was very dark, but the crumb is very very gummy. Flavor around crust is good but texture is bad.
- Banneton has a ton of dough attached and now needs a deep clean

Take aways
- Use plastic container w/ markings for bulk proof to measure % rise
- Ensure that starter is very active for use. It should be able to 2x-3x w/i 4-5 hours at a regular feeding.
