recipe:
  prep_time: "10 minutes"
  cook_time: "10 minutes"
  yield: "4 servings"
  ingredient_groups:
    - items:
        - "1 lb ground chicken"
        - "4 cloves garlic, minced"
        - "3–4 Thai bird's eye chilies, sliced"
        - "1 cup Thai holy basil leaves (or regular basil)"
        - "2 tbsp soy sauce"
        - "1 tbsp oyster sauce"
        - "1 tbsp fish sauce"
        - "1 tsp sugar"
        - "2 tbsp vegetable oil"
        - "Steamed jasmine rice, for serving"
  step_groups:
    - steps:
        - "Heat oil in a wok over **high heat** until smoking."
        - "Add garlic and chilies — stir for 30 seconds."
        - "Add chicken, breaking it up. Cook until no longer pink, about 4 minutes."
        - "Stir in soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Toss to combine."
        - "Kill the heat, fold in the basil until just wilted."
        - "Serve over jasmine rice with a fried egg on top if you like."
---

## Notes

> Holy basil (bai krapao) has a peppery, clove-like flavor that's different from sweet basil. If you can find it at an Asian grocery store, it makes a huge difference.
