recipe:
  ingredient_groups:
    - items:
        - "1/2 cup (113g) lukewarm milk"
        - "3 large eggs, at room temperature"
        - "heaping 1/2 teaspoon table salt"
        - "1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar"
        - "4 tablespoons (57g) butter, softened"
        - "2 cups (240g) King Arthur Unbleached All-Purpose Flour"
        - "2 teaspoons instant yeast"
        - "1/4 cup (43g) currants or raisins, golden raisins preferred"
        - "1/4 cup (43g) candied mixed peel, or mixed dried fruit, chopped"
    - name: "Rum syrup"
      items:
        - "1/2 cup (99g) granulated sugar"
        - "1/4 cup (57g) water"
        - "1 to 2 tablespoons (14g to 28g) rum"
    - name: "Icing, optional"
      items:
        - "1 cup (113g) confectioners' sugar"
        - "pinch of table salt"
        - "2 tablespoons (28g) milk, or a combination of milk and rum or apple juice"
  step_groups:
    - steps:
        - "Weigh the flour, or measure it by gently spooning it into a cup and sweeping off any excess. Place everything except the fruit in a mixing bowl, and beat at medium speed until cohesive. Increase the mixer speed to high, and beat for 2 minutes."
        - "Add the fruit and beat gently just to combine."
        - "Cover the bowl and let the dough rest/rise for 60 minutes. It won't appear to do too much."
        - "Scoop the batter into a greased 10-cup Bundt pan. Cover the pan and let the dough rest/rise for 30 minutes while preheating the oven to 350F."
        - "Bake for 35 to 40 minutes, until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center reads at least 190F."
        - "While the babka bakes, prepare the rum syrup. Combine the syrup ingredients in a small saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil and boil, swirling the pan, until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat."
        - "Remove the babka from the oven. Poke it all over gently with a toothpick or fork, then slowly pour the syrup over the surface."
        - "Once the syrup is absorbed, about 20 minutes, carefully loosen the edges and turn the babka out onto a rack."
        - "If using the icing, mix all of the ingredients until smooth and drizzle over completely cool babka."
---

While KA calls this a babka, it is spiritually much closer to a savarin in character due to the low enrichment, reliance of a syrup, batter bread, and single rise.
