  When you want something that feels fancy but takes 25 minutes, this is it.
recipe:
  prep_time: "10 minutes"
  cook_time: "15 minutes"
  yield: "4 servings"
  ingredient_groups:
    - items:
        - "12 oz linguine"
        - "1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined"
        - "4 tbsp unsalted butter"
        - "5 cloves garlic, thinly sliced"
        - "1/4 cup dry white wine"
        - "Juice of 1 lemon"
        - "Red pepper flakes"
        - "Fresh parsley, chopped"
        - "Salt and pepper"
        - "Parmesan, for serving"
  step_groups:
    - steps:
        - "Cook linguine in salted water according to package directions. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining."
        - "Pat shrimp dry, season with salt and pepper."
        - "Melt 2 tbsp butter in a large skillet over **medium-high heat**. Sear shrimp 1–2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside."
        - "Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining butter and garlic — cook until fragrant, about 1 minute."
        - "Pour in white wine and lemon juice, scraping up any browned bits."
        - "Add the drained pasta and a splash of pasta water. Toss until coated."
        - "Return the shrimp to the pan. Add red pepper flakes and parsley. Toss once more."
        - "Serve with freshly grated Parmesan."
---

> The reserved pasta water is liquid gold — the starch helps the sauce cling to every strand.
